Learning NixOS

Having used Linux for about 4 years now. I started with Arch, and quickly realized that was a bad decision, and retreated to Fedora. Fedora worked fine until I wanted to do anything with CUDA, for some reason I could not get this to work consistently, and out of frustration switched to Pop OS. Pop OS was the first time I felt comfortable installing and updating, feeling like most essential software would not break.

Pop OS was working well, until I got a new desktop. Now every time I installed a new package or program, there would be a difference between my laptop and desktop. Furthermore, as I started to branch out of simple python development and integrate multiple languages I noticed programs would fail to run on other machines. This later issue might be fixed by using something like Docker, but in my never ending yearning to change my computing environments I wanted something more. I wanted to know about every program installed on my computer. I wanted to run NixOS
